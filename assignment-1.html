The cell cycle is a fundamental process that occurs in all somatic cells, involving a series of events leading to cell division. Let’s explore its key stages: Interphase: G1 (Gap 1) Phase: During G1, the cell grows, copies organelles, and prepares molecular building blocks for later steps. S (Synthesis) Phase: In S phase, the cell synthesizes a complete copy of its DNA and duplicates the centrosome (which helps separate DNA during M phase). G2 (Gap 2) Phase: In G2, the cell continues to grow, produces proteins and organelles, and reorganizes its contents for mitosis. Mitotic (M) Phase: Mitosis: The cell separates its duplicated DNA into two sets, forming two identical daughter cells. Cytokinesis: The cell divides its cytoplasm, resulting in two new cells. Remember that the cell cycle is a cycle, allowing daughter cells to start the process anew after each division. Animal and plant cells follow similar principles, with additional phases like G0 (a resting state) and G1 checkpoint ensuring proper progression.
